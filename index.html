Exercise Library

Cole Lindfors

Pull - Movement 1

1. Seated Cable Row

While seated at the machine, pull the cable towards your chest while keeping your back straight.

2. Lat Pulldown

While seated at the machine, pull the cable down below your chin. Refrain from leaning back to emphasize lats.

3. One Arm Dumbbell Row

Putting one hand on a bench while leaning over, grab a dumbbell with the other hand and pull it towards your chest.

4. Dumbbell Bent Reverse Grip Row

Bend over with two dumbbells while keeping your back straight, and pull towards your chest.

5. Reverse Fly

Bend over with two dumbbells while keeping your back straight, and raise your arms out to the side.

6. Barbell Bent Row

Bend over the barbell while keeping your back straight. Grab it with an overhand grip and pull the barbell towards your chest.

7. Yates Row

Similar to the barbell row, except with an underhand grip on the bar.

8. Pendlay Row

Similar to the barbell row, except for every rep you hit the ground with the barbell.

9. Cable Face Pull

While standing, grab the cable and pull it to your face, bringing your hands above your shoulders.

10. Inverted Row

Setting up the inverted row, make sure the bar is in the corner of the safety bar and the rack. When positioning yourself on the ground, position so you can pull diagonally upwards towards the bar.

Squat - Movement 2

1. Barbell Squat

Facing the rack, pick up the barbell at a height below your natural shoulder height. Position your feet at a neutral position and squat, then stand up driving through your heels.

2. Single Leg Pistol Squat

Standing on one leg, drop into a squat with your other leg and arms extended. This advanced exercise goes down very deep, ending at nearly a sit on your squatting leg. Stand up on the same leg after maximum depth is achieved.

3. Single Leg Bench Squat

Similar to the pistol squat. This easier single-leg exercise does not go as deep, and you can sit down (easier) or tap (more difficult) the bench before standing up again.

4. Goblet Squat

Pick up a dumbbell and hold it by the end as pictured below. After standing in a neutral position, proceed with the squat. The dumbbell can help balance you out to prevent tipping backward in a deep squat.

5. Kettlebell Sumo Squat

Similar to the goblet squat, on this squat, you hold a kettlebell with your hands level to your chest. In this squat your stance is wider, hence the sumo squat.

6. Hack Squat

Climb into the machine and align shoulders to drive up into the pads with hands on the grips and feet planted. Then push up using your legs to raise the weights and back down for a rep.

7. Leg Press

Rest back against the pad and grip handles, with feet planted on board. Push legs outward without locking out knees, then bring them back.

8. Barbell Overhead Squat

Raise the bar above your head and proceed with a standard squat while maintaining balance. This advanced exercise engages lots of muscles to stabilize the bar above the head.

9. Jefferson Squat

Stand above the barbell, straddling the bar. With the hand on the side of your leading leg, grab with an overhand grip. With the other hand, grab with an underhand grip. Bring the bar up to between your legs then set it down, while maintaining a straight and vertical back.

10. Barbell Front Squat

Position yourself towards the rack, and place the barbell on the front of your shoulders. Brace arms diagonally up to the bar, and grip. Proceed with a squat motion in a neutral position.

Lunge - Movement 3

1. Lunge (Bodyweight)

Standing vertically in a neutral position, step forward with one leg and bring that thigh parallel to the ground. The back legs knee should be near (or touching) the ground with the thigh perpendicular to the ground and the shin parallel. Stand back up using power through your front foot.

2. Barbell Lunge

With a barbell on your shoulders, proceed with a normal lunge while stabilizing the barbell.

3. Dumbbell Lunge

With dumbbells hanging at a neutral position in each hand, proceed with a normal lunge.

4. Walking Lunge

Similar to the bodyweight lunge, you keep moving forward in this exercise by bringing your back leg in front after standing back up. This makes the lunge more of a dynamic exercise.

5. Dumbbell Reverse Lunge

Similar to the dumbbell lunge, this exercise steps backward instead of forwards for the lunge. This often results in a better lunge, but you must make sure your surroundings are clear.

6. Side Lunge

This lunge starts in a neutral position but lunges to the side instead of forward. This results in your lunging leg being moved outward, resulting in your other leg being fully extended. Stepping back up is done by pushing through the lunging leg.

7. Dumbbell Side Lunge

This lunge is similar to the side lunge but with extra weight from a dumbbell. After holding the dumbbell like in the goblet squat, a side lunge is performed.

8. Kettlebell Lunge

This lunge is similar to the bodyweight lunge, but with added weight from kettlebells. Holding a kettlebell in each hand at a neutral hanging position, a standard lunge is performed.

9. Dumbbell Curtsy Lunge

This lunge is similar to the dumbbell lunge. After picking up the dumbbell in the goblet position, you step backward diagonally with one leg. To push up, use the front leg.

10. Barbell Side Lunge

This exercise combines aspects of the barbell lunge and the side lunge. This is a difficult exercise to maintain the stability of the barbell, along with the added weight.

Plank - Movement 4

1. Elbow Plank

Lie face down on the ground. Then raise and rest on elbows with forearms forward, maintaining a straight posture. Keep your glutes up slightly to avoid putting extra stress on your lower back.

2. Weighted Elbow Plank

Similar to the elbow plank, except with added weight. This can be achieved easily by placing a plate on your lower back before starting the workout.

3. Bird Dog

After getting on hands and knees, raise your opposite arm and leg and hold. Keep arm and leg level with the rest of your body.

4. Side Plank

On the ground, place one forearm with palm flat and plank on one side. Keep above foot level with your foot on the ground, and hold your above arm tight to your body.

5. Dead Bug

Lying with your back on the ground, raise your knees to vertical and arms straight out. This position can be held, or alternating legs can be lowered to level with the ground repeatedly.

6. Leg Raise (Dip Machine)

At a dip machine, place forearms on pads and hands on grips. Then raise straight legs outwards til level with the ground (if possible).

7. Lying Leg Raise

Lying on your back, raise your straight legs til perpendicular to the ground (if possible). Use arms to stabilize your body.

8. Hanging Leg Raise

Hanging from a pull-up rack, raise straight legs to parallel with the ground.

9. Plank With Leg Lift

Similar to the standard elbow plank, this plank requires one leg to be elevated raising it.

10. Pushup plank

Similar to the elbow plank, but instead of being on your elbows you are on your palms with your arms fully extended.

Hinge - Movement 5

1. Nordic Hamstring

Either in a machine or with a partner holding your ankles, get on your knees and start vertically. With arms toward your chest, decline your chest towards the ground using your legs to hold you up. If possible, go back up to vertical. If not, fall into a push-up position and repeat.

2. Kettlebell Deadlift

Standing at a neutral position, lower to the ground by sticking out your glutes and keeping a straight back. Knee bend is natural from sticking out glutes. This is where the rep begins. Grab the kettlebell by the handle, stand up and keep back straight. Lower kettlebell to ground for a rep.

3. Dumbbell Deadlift

This exercise is similar to the kettlebell deadlift, except is executed with two dumbbells. These dumbbells are held close to the shin while going up and down, keeping the extra weight close to your center of mass.

4. Barbell Deadlift

This exercise is similar to the dumbbell deadlift, except with a barbell. Once again make sure to keep the bar close to your shins while raising and lowering the weight. One benefit to the barbell is the height off the ground it starts at, so you don't need to go so far down to the ground.

5. Single Leg Dumbbell Deadlift

This exercise is similar to the dumbbell deadlift but instead is done using one leg for power. The second leg is used to maintain balance, so it is stuck out backward for stability.

6. Romanian Barbell Deadlift

The Romanian barbell deadlift is similar to the barbell deadlift, except it starts at a standing position. In this lift, you do not set the weight down and keep straighter legs. This results in more effort from different muscles. At the bottom of a rep, your back should be close to parallel with the ground before standing back up.

7. Hip Bridge

The hip bridge is started by lying on your back, with your knees up and feet planted halfway to your glutes. You then raise your hips and make your chest level with your thighs, pause a moment, then lower yourself back down. You can use your hands on the sides for stability if needed.

8. Cable Pull Through

Standing with your back to the machine, hold the cable between your legs, bent over with straight legs and a straight back. Pull the cable through your legs, bringing your body to an upright neutral position. bring the cable back down for one rep.

9. Lying Hamstring Curl

Lying face down on the machine with arms on the pad and hands gripping handles. Tuck your legs under the leg pad before you begin. Engage your Hamstrings and pull your heels towards your body, hinging at the knee.

10. Seated Hamstring Curl

Sit at the machine, with the thigh pad pressed up to the thighs (adjust the machine if needed), and the back of the ankle on the other pad. Hinge at the knee and bring heels towards your body.

Rotation - Movement 6

1. Kettlebell Russian Twist

This ab exercise is performed sitting on the ground with back up at a diagonal incline. Holding the kettlebell, twist your core and move the weight across your body back and forth.

2. Pallof Press

This exercise is performed at a cable machine with a mid-height cable. Standing perpendicular to the machine, hold the cable near your core. Press the cable away from your body and away from the machine using your arms, and engage your core to resist rotation.

3. Pallof Hold

Similar to the pallof press, this exercise is done by holding the cable at an extended position. Instead of moving it back towards your core, maintain a long hold resisting rotation.

4. Wood Chop (Cable)

The wood chop is a rotational movement engaging lots of muscles in the body. Utilizing a cable system with a high-mounted cable, the exercise begins at the top and is done by pulling the cable across the body diagonally downwards.

Push - Movement 7

1. Barbell Bench Press

This pushing exercise begins lying on the back under a rack. After picking up the barbell at shoulder width grip, make sure it is lined up with the center of your upper chest and lower and raise the bar.

2. Dumbbell Bench Press

Similar to the barbell bench press, this exercise is done with dumbbells instead of a barbell. This adds another element of stability and isolates each arm.

3. Incline Dumbbell Bench Press

This exercise is similar to the dumbbell bench press, except is done with an inclined bench. This puts more stress on your upper pec than the standard dumbbell bench press.

4. Dumbbell Hex Press

This exercise is similar to the dumbbell bench press. The difference is that the dumbbells are held vertically instead of horizontally, and they are pressed together throughout the movement. It is best done with hex-shaped dumbbells, so you can push together flat sides.

5. Dumbbell Military Press

This exercise is similar to the incline dumbbell bench press, but you are seated completely straight instead of on an incline. This exercise primarily works the shoulders.

6. Seated Pec Fly

For this exercise, sit with your back on the pad for this machine. Grabbing the handles, bring your hands together with your arms fully extended for one rep.

7. Pec Deck

Similar to the seated pec fly, this exercise uses similar muscles but is done on a different machine with pads for the area around your elbow. Gripping the top of the handles, bring your elbows together.

8. Dips

Climb into the dip machine and sit with your arms extended at the top of a dip. Lower down til your upper arm is near parallel with the ground (if possible), then raise back up for one rep.

9. Push-up

Lying on the ground face down, place hands shoulder width apart and push up then decline back down for one rep. Keep your back and legs straight during this exercise.

10. Diamond Push-Up

Similar to the push-up, this exercise has a different hand placement. Instead of a wider stance with your hands, place your hands together in the middle and form a diamond shape in the gap. Proceed with the rest of the regular pushup form with this modification.
